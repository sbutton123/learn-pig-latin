🌍 Welcome to Piglatinia

🌀 Language & Life

  • Pig Latin is the native language. It’s spoken at home, in schools, on government forms, and in bedtime lullabies.
  • Visitors may be confused. Locals won’t notice.
  • Most Piglatinians aren’t aware their language is unusual elsewhere.
  • “Itway ustjay akesmay ensesay otay usyay.”

🏛️ Capital City: Atinlay Ouncay

  • The Spiral Tower of Loops
  • The annual Flip-Your-Words Festival
  • Public transit by hula-hooping roller skaters

💃 Traditions & National Pastimes

🌀 Hula Hooping
More than just a sport—used in meditation, education, debates, and fire drills.

🎪 Juggling
Introduced by Gregor from “somewhere over there.” Now the national art of “multi-ring pigformance.”

🎓 Education

  • Spinning Arts
  • Facial Expression Theory
  • Syllable Stretching
  • Reversal Rhetoric
  • Lunch (with glitter)

📅 Holidays

  • Oopay Ayday — Celebrate the invention of the hula hoop.
  • Word Flip Week — All words reversed twice for comedy.
  • JuggleHoopmas — Lights, scarves, snacks, and hoop juggling.

👗 Fashion & Style

  • Clothing must allow for emergency hooping.
  • Bright, clashing colors are encouraged.
  • Spinning accessories are considered classy.

🧭 Tourism Tips

  • Never try to out‑hoop a local.
  • Accept glitter with grace.
  • If handed a juggling ball, just roll with it.

🧾 Citizenship

  • Pass the 5‑hoop spin challenge.
  • Translate your name into Pig Latin.
  • Tell a joke that makes a native laugh.
