What is Pig Latin?

Pig Latin is a language that originates from Piglatinia . Pig Latin has spread to America and has become a playful secret code where English words are altered in fun ways. It was popular among kids for making conversations sound silly or private! While it's a popular rumor that Thomas Jefferson used Pig Latin in his letters, it's not definitively proven.

How to Translate:

  • If a word starts with a vowel (a, e, i, o, u), just add "yay" or "way" to the end. Example: "apple" → "appleyay" . In Piglatinia, Piglatinians add either "way" or "yay" depending on whether they come from the East or West side of Piglatinia.
  • If it starts with a consonant or consonant cluster, move it to the end and add "ay" . Example: "hello" → "ellohay"
  • Punctuation and capitalization are preserved!
